Features

Every tool your PI team uses, in one place

Built by a public involvement professional, for public involvement professionals. No generic CRM workarounds — just the workflow you already know, properly supported.

Stakeholder registry

A master contact list across all your projects. Link stakeholders to projects, assign roles and influence tiers, and carry engagement history forward automatically.

Interaction logging

Log every phone call, email, field visit, and meeting. Track channel, sentiment, and who logged it — from the office or from the field on your phone.

Commitments tracker

Every commitment your team makes gets logged, assigned, and tracked to resolution. Nothing falls through the cracks when the project runs long.

PI report generation

Generate branded Word documents — PI reports, issues summaries, and stakeholder matrices — from your live data. What used to take half a day takes minutes.

Mobile field app

Your field team logs contacts and interactions on any phone — no app install required. Everything syncs instantly with the desktop registry.

Project dashboard

At-a-glance project health: engagement pace, open commitments, sentiment trends, deliverable status, and stakeholder engagement matrix — all in one view.

How it works

From first contact to final report

01

Set up your project

Add your project, import your existing contact list, and link stakeholders in minutes. Duplicate detection keeps your registry clean from day one.

02

Log every interaction

Desktop or mobile — your team logs calls, emails, and field contacts as they happen. Every interaction is tied to a stakeholder, a project, and a date.

03

Track issues and commitments

When a concern is raised or a commitment made, log it instantly. Assign follow-up, set categories, and track resolution without losing context.

04

Generate your PI report

At any point, generate a fully formatted report — narrative sections, interaction logs, commitment status, and stakeholder matrix — ready to submit.

Meet the founder

A career built on telling complex stories simply

Jeff Putzke
Founder · Public Involvement Services Manager
Sunrise Engineering, Inc.
LinkedIn →

Horizon COMPASS exists because Jeff Putzke has lived every side of public communication — and saw firsthand how much of it still runs on spreadsheets, sticky notes, and tribal knowledge. His career started not in stakeholder engagement, but in code. As a computer programmer for St. Paul Regional Water Services in the mid-1990s, Jeff learned how to design systems that solve real, repeatable problems. That foundation in software thinking never left him.

From there, his path took an unusual turn. Armed with a Bachelor of Science in Meteorology from St. Cloud State University, Jeff spent the next 15+ years as a broadcast meteorologist across six major television markets — Minneapolis, Omaha, Traverse City, Salt Lake City (KTVX ABC4), Tucson (Chief Meteorologist, KGUN 9), and Sacramento (KOVR/CBS) — and continued as a freelance meteorologist in the Salt Lake market through 2022. Live television taught him what an audience needs: clear narratives, plain language, and the ability to distill complex technical information into something a viewer cares about in under thirty seconds. Mass engagement, public speaking, and storytelling design became second nature.

In 2019, Jeff joined Sunrise Engineering and built the Public Involvement practice from a one-person specialty into a full team serving UDOT, municipal, and utility projects across Utah. Along the way, he became a certified PublicInput partner and worked on dozens of high-profile transportation and infrastructure communications efforts. Horizon COMPASS is the natural product of that arc — software engineering instincts, broadcast-honed clarity, and a deep understanding of what PI teams actually do every day, built into one tool by someone who has lived the work.
